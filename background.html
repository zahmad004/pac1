Background

What is the PAC?

Established by Executive Order 13467, the Security, Suitability, and Credentialing Performance Accountability Council (more commonly known as the PAC) is accountable to the President for driving the implementation of personnel vetting reform initiatives, advising the Executive Agents on vetting policy, monitoring the performance of vetting practices, and identifying enterprise-level process enhancements among other responsibilities.


What is personnel reform vetting?

“Personnel vetting” is a broad term used to describe the myriad of ways the Government evaluates its Federal and contractor workforces for trustworthiness. The PAC PMO focuses on three categories of vetting:

  1. Security Clearance
    The process used to determine trustworthiness to access classified information.
  2. Suitability & Fitness
    The process used to determine whether personnel are adequately trustworthy to perform the type of work required for a position.
  3. Credentialing
    The process used to determine whether personnel are adequately trustworthy to be issued a badge, typically granting physical access to a facility.

Why is personnel reform important?

The world continues to change at a quickening pace, requiring the SSC community to anticipate, detect, and counter both internal and external threats. SSC methods have struggled to keep pace with constantly evolving threats and new technological capabilities allow insiders to cause exponentially more harm than what was possible 60 years ago.

While there have been considerable reform achievements, there has been intense scrutiny from Congress and an erosion of the public’s confidence in the Federal Government’s ability to properly vet its workforce due to cybersecurity breaches and significant investigation backlogs.

Key Organizations

  • Suitability and Credentialing Executive Agent (SuitEA/CredEA)
    The Suit/CredEA is responsible for the development, implementation, and oversight of policies and procedures governing the conduct of investigations and adjudications for suitability, fitness, and credentialing. The Director of the Office of Personnel Management (OPM) serves as the Suit/CredEA.


  • Enterprise Investment Board (EIB)
    The EIB is an executive steering committee established to help the PAC manage personnel vetting investments across the Executive Branch, particularly regarding information technologies. It is Co-Chaired by OMB, OPM, and Deputy Federal Chief Information Officer. Together, EIB member agencies represent 90% of all government’s personnel vetting resources and funding. Member agencies include DoD, DHS, DOJ, DOE, ODNI, OPM, Treasury, State, GSA, and NBIB.
  • Security Executive Agent (SecEA)
    The SecEA is responsible for the development, implementation, and oversight of policies and procedures governing the conduct of investigations and adjudications for eligibility for access to classified information and eligibility to hold a sensitive position. The Director of National Intelligence serves as the SecEA.

  • Performance Accountability Council Program Management
    The PMO assists the PAC’s leadership in implementing its mission priorities. The organization is funded by PAC member contributions and staffed with subject matter experts. It is responsible for many of the PAC’s Reform Initiatives, and serves in a coordination role for interagency efforts.
